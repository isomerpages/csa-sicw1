<p>The milestone 10th edition of the Singapore International Cyber Week came
at a critical global juncture. Intensifying geopolitical competition, shifting
dynamics in global governance, and the growing scale of cyber threats continuously
reshape the digital and cyber landscape.</p>
<p>Under the theme “Shaping the Next Era of Global Cybersecurity”, SICW 2025
explored the impact of cyber developments on international security and
the digital economy. It focused on the shifting global environment and
reaffirmed the vital role of having an inclusive dialogue in shaping the
next era of global cybersecurity.&nbsp;</p>
<p>SICW continues to serve as a premier global platform for policymakers,
diplomats, technical experts, industry leaders, and academics to drive
conversations on cyber and digital security. The 2025 edition addressed
a wide spectrum of issues – from cyber diplomacy and international cooperation,
to emerging technologies such as AI and quantum, and the development of
a trusted and secure digital ecosystem.</p>
<p>Key SICW 2025 events included the <strong>SICW Summit</strong>, <strong>Opening Plenary Session: High-Level Panels</strong>,
a series of <strong>SICW High-Level Panels </strong>focusing on diverse
cybersecurity diplomacy, policy, operational and technical topics, as well
as the <strong>Women in Cyber</strong> plenary and <strong>AI Security Roundtable</strong>.
Other various high-level events included the 10th ASEAN Ministerial Conference
on Cybersecurity (AMCC), the ASEAN Cybercrime Prosecutors’ Roundtable Meeting
(ACPRM), and the 11th ASEAN Senior Officials Roundtable on Cybercrime (SORC).</p>
<p>SICW 2025 presented the <strong><a href="http://www.govware.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">GovWare Conference and Exhibition</a></strong> as
its anchor trade show. Asia’s premier cybersecurity platform, GovWare 2025
featured world-class keynotes, specialised conference tracks, and sector-focused
CII Forums for Financial Services and Healthcare, offering expert insights
and critical dialogues among policymakers, business leaders, and solution
providers. Other highlights included the Singapore Government Closed-Door
Session, executive roundtables, and over 300 international exhibitors showcasing
cutting-edge solutions from AI security to zero trust architectures. The
GovWare Exhibition also featured international pavilions and dedicated
showcases spotlighting breakthrough technologies and secure digital solutions
from infrastructure leaders, along with Tech Talk sessions delivering practical
intelligence and live technical demonstrations.</p>
